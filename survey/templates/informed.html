PARTICIPANT INFORMED CONSENT FORM

The following information is provided to inform you about the research project and your participation in it. Your participation in this research study is voluntary. You are free to withdraw from this study at any time without penalty.

  1. Purpose of the study:

    You are being asked to participate in an academic survey about Knock Codes, a passcode interface on LG smartphones for locking and unlocking the device, like PINs or Android Patterns. We are interested in learning how users select and remember knock codes.

  2. Description of procedures to be followed and approximate time duration involved for the participants:

    You will be asked to complete a short survey that requires you to generate a set of Knock Codes under a security scenario, such as locking your device or protecting your bank account.

    A Knock Code is an mobile authentication system used to lock/unlock LG smartphone devices. If you are unfamiliar with Knock Codes, we will explain them to you in more detail in the course of the survey.

    The expect time of the survey is 10 minutes, and no more than 1 hour. You will be compensated $1.25 for your participation.

  3. Expected costs :

    There are no expected costs to your to participation.

  4. Description of the discomfort, inconveniences, and/or risks that can be reasonably expected as a result of participation in this study:

    There are no foreseeable risks for participating in this research.

  5. Anticipated benefits from this study:

    The benefits of participation are to further research in mobile authentication, which in part will improve the security of mobile device users.

  6. Compensation for participation:

    You will be compensated $1.25 for an honest completion of this survey.

  7. Circumstances under which the Principal Investigator may withdraw you from study participation:

    Your results may be withdrawn or rejected from this survey for the following reasons: i) Not following directions of the survey ii) Providing an invalid completion code iii) Failing attention tests and screening questions that render you unqualified

  8. What happens if you choose to withdraw from study participation:

    You may withdraw from study participation at any point prior to submitting your completion code, without prejudice. You may either opt-out of the survey or return the HIT. Participation will be considered incomplete if you abandon the HIT.
